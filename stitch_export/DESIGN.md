## Overview

A hyperlocal community platform for liquor enthusiasts. The interface seamlessly blends an approachable, warm neighborhood aesthetic with a premium, focused environment for liquor exploration. It features a welcoming light mode for community interactions and a sophisticated dark mode for focused browsing and reviews.

## Colors

- **Primary** (#FF7E36): CTAs, active states, key interactive elements. Conveys a warm, inviting community feel.

- **Secondary** (#1A1A1A): Supporting actions, premium surfaces, and deep backgrounds for the dark theme.

- **Tertiary** (#D9A05B): Accent highlights, premium badges, and tasting note tags.

- **Neutral** (#868E96): Subtitles, helper text, borders, and non-chromatic UI elements.

## Typography

- **Headline Font**: Public Sans

- **Body Font**: Public Sans

- **Label Font**: Public Sans

Headlines use bold weight to establish clear hierarchy. Body text uses regular weight at 14-16px for readability in community posts and tasting notes.

Labels use medium weight at 12px for metadata such as ABV, volume, and location tags.

## Elevation

The design relies primarily on surface color variation and subtle borders for depth rather than heavy shadows. Cards and modals use a very soft, diffuse shadow only to lift interactive elements off the background, maintaining a clean and modern aesthetic.

## Components

- **Buttons**: Fully rounded for primary actions to feel approachable, using the primary orange fill. Secondary buttons use an outline style with neutral borders.

- **Cards**: Maximum, pill-shaped roundedness to maintain a friendly look. Community posts use flat surfaces with 1px neutral borders. Liquor detail cards utilize subtle elevation.

- **Chips**: Used heavily for filtering liquor categories and neighborhood areas. Maximum, pill-shaped roundedness, surface-variant background for unselected, primary background for selected.

- **Inputs**: Maximum, pill-shaped roundedness, filled surface-variant background without borders, bold active state border using the primary color.

## Do's and Don'ts

- Do ensure the primary orange color is used sparingly in dark mode to avoid visual fatigue.

- Don't use sharp corners on interactive elements; maintain the friendly, rounded geometry throughout.

- Do provide high contrast for text over dark surfaces to ensure readability of tasting notes and reviews.

- Don't mix playful typography with the premium liquor detail pages; keep the font presentation structured and clean.
